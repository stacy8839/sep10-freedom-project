Veterinary Technology

Technology plays a major role in modern veterinary medicine. Apps, tools, and machines help veterinarians improve accuracy, communication, patient monitoring, emergency care, and diagnostic precision.

Ever since I was young, I have felt a deep connection to animals and have grown up surrounded by them. I have always felt a strong responsibility to care for them and make sure they are healthy and safe. About four years ago, I traveled to Mexico during my sixth-grade summer break. While I was there, I used my own lunch money to buy food because it was more affordable, and I used that money to feed homeless dogs, cats, and even roosters in my neighborhood. My grandmother also owned horses, and whenever I visited, I would help take care of them by giving them baths and making sure they were well cared for. These experiences had a strong impact on me and helped me realize how much I truly want to become a veterinarian. To this day, I continue to care for and help animals whenever I can. My goal is to use my passion and knowledge to improve the lives of animals and make a meaningful difference in my community.

Veterinary Apps

VetCalculator
  • Quick dosage calculator for vets and vet students
  • Prevents medication errors
  • Works offline
  • Free to downloads
  • Designed for clinical/emergency accuracy.
  • It performs one basic task (dose calculations) using simple formulas. No data storage, communication, or advanced systems involved.
Pet Poison Helpline
  • Gives info about toxic foods, plants, and chemicals.
  • Included pictures of common toxins
  • Large searchable toxin database
  • Offers emergency steps
  • Works on phones and tablets
  • It provides information only. It's helpful but still an informational tool, not a medical system.
PetDesk
  • Connects pet owners to their vet clinic
  • Appointment booking & reminders
  • Stores medical and vaccination records
  • Supports multiple pets
  • Improves vet-owner communication!
  • It handles schedules, records, notifications, and communication, and even more features.

Veterinary Hardware

Autoclave
  • Sterilizes tools using high-pressure steam
  • Automatic drying feautures
  • Eco-friendly and safe for all patients
  • Some models connect to Wi-Fi
  • Ensures zero contamination between animals
  • The machine uses mechanical parts, pressure, heat, timing, and sometimes digital monitor.
Intravenous (IV) Pumps
  • Delivers accurate, controlled medication and fluid flow
  • Prevents dosage mistakes
  • Lightweight and portable
  • Battery operated
  • Some models save dosage data
  • Alerts vets when doses are complete
  • Includes programmable settings, sensors, data storage, and sometimes wireless connectivity.
Magnetic resonance imaging(MRI)
  • Uses Strong magnets and radio waves to create internal images
  • Gives clearer results than X-rays or CT scans
  • Detects tumors, joint issues, and brain problems
  • Helps vets diagnose without surgery
  • Assists in monitoring recovery and surgical progress
  • MRI machines use advanced imaging software, computer systems, magnet technology, and detailed data processing. It is the highest level of veterinary equipment.

My Inventions

These innovations combine technology and veterinary care to improve diagnosis, treatment, and communication.

HoloVet Scanner

  • Creates 3D holograms of an animal's internal organs without surgery.
  • Uses advanced sensors to scan through tissue safely.
  • Helps vets diagnose injuries faster and with more accuracy.

    • HOLO-VET SCANNER

    A scanner that creates 3D images of an animal’s internal organs, helping veterinarians diagnose conditions quickly and without surgery.

    SMARTBANDAGE

    An advanced bandage that monitors wounds in real time, detecting infection and sending alerts to veterinarians to improve healing.

    PET EMOTIONS TRANSLATOR

    A device that analyzes body signals and heart rate to understand an animal’s emotions, helping both vets and animals with better and more comfortable care.
